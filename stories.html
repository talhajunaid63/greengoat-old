The Boston Children's Museum used to be a factory back in the day. About halfway through the project, I spied some original fire shutters leaning against the dumpster. I'm often struck by the beauty of well worn materials. Like the perfect pair of jeans, a thrashed door with peeling paint tells the story of passing years and ... persistence, I guess.

These shutters were rusted through, and the red paint had faded to a nice, weatherbeaten persimmon. I asked the site super for a few days on them, and began making calls. After a few strike outs with local sculptors, I remembered the set design warehouse for the ART across from Anderson McQuaid in Cambridge. I got the name of the set design director and dialed.

"Hi ... this is Amy from greenGoat. I was wondering whether you'd like some industrial 'castle doors' for an upcoming production. I thought they might make good props for an updated version of Hamlet or something."

ART sent a truck over the next day, and I could not have been prouder to see a few pieces of our industrial legacy live on. So much of it has drifted away. I wonder if the shutters ever made it on stage.
